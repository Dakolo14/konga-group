Our Timeline

  3. Our Timeline

2012

Launch and Early Adoption

JULY 2012
Konga.com was launched with a bold vision to simplify the retail experience, and revolutionise online shopping in Nigeria.

The same day the website went live, Kehinde Laleye placed the first order on Konga.com for a beauty product. Watch Here

JULY 2013
Konga launched its first three pickup centres in key areas of Lagos: Victoria Island, Surulere, and Ikeja.

NOVEMBER 2013
Konga.com crashed for 45 minutes on November 29, 2013, due to unprecedented traffic driven by its Black Friday promotion.

2013

Rapid Growth Phase

2014

Rapid Growth Phase

JANUARY 2014
Konga raised $25 million in Series B funding, marking one of the largest investments ever received by an African startup at that time.

DECEMBER 2014
Konga's Marketplace featured 8,000 merchants

JANUARY 2015
Konga was ranked as the most visited website in Nigeria by Alexa Internet.

JUNE 2015
Konga acquired the assets and MMO licence of Zinternet Nigeria Limited to commence mobile payment services.

AUGUST 2015
Konga launched KongaPay to offer safe, reliable, and seamless payment solutions for Konga’s merchants and customers.

2013

Rapid Growth Phase

2016

Building Customer Loyalty

APRIL 2016
Konga introduced KOS Logistics and Same-Day Delivery option to provide customers with a faster, more dependable shopping experience.

FEBRUARY 2018
Konga was acquired by the Zinox Group, laying the foundation for Konga’s future expansion.

MARCH 2018
Nick Imudia joined Konga and was appointed by the Board as CEO.

APRIL 2018
Prince Nnamdi Ekeh, founder of Yudala, is appointed Co-CEO of Konga, to lead alongside Nick.

2018

Konga Acquisiton by Zinox Group

2018

Merger with Yudala

MAY 2018
Konga was merged with Zinox Group’s retail company, Yudala, Nigeria’s first omnichannel retail platform founded in November 2014. A new company logo and identity was formed.

The months that followed allowed for the integration of both companies' logistics, technology, and customer service infrastructures, broadened Konga’s market reach into previously underserved regions by leveraging Yudala’s existing customer base.

MARCH 2019
Konga launched Konga Travel and Tours, an IATA-certified travel agency. This diversification allowed Konga to extend its services beyond retail and into the travel sector, offering Nigerian travellers a reliable platform for booking flights, hotels, and holiday packages at competitive rates.

2019

Diversifying the Portfolio

2021

Diversifying the Portfolio

JUNE 2021
Konga continued its expansion into new verticals with the launch of Konga Health, a health-tech distribution company. In partnership with the Association of Nigerian Private Medical Practitioners (ANPMP), Konga Health aimed to enhance access to quality healthcare products and services across Nigeria.

SEPTEMBER 2022
Nick Imudia stepped down as Co-CEO, and Prince Nnamdi Ekeh took over as sole CEO of Konga Group

2022

Continued Growth

2024

Strategic Partnerships

JANUARY 2024
Konga acquired TDiLife, further diversifying its portfolio with an expansion into the FMCG distribution sector.

JULY 2024
Konga Health formed a strategic exclusive partnership with global beauty brand L’Oreal to launch its popular skincare line, CeraVe, in Nigeria.

SEPTEMBER 2024
KongaHealth expanded its reseller network for L’Oreal’s beauty products, including renowned brands like CeraVe, La Roche-Posay, and Garnier
